Jagannath Temple


  • Jagannath Temple

    • About Jagannath Temple:

      • The Jagannath Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Jagannath, a form of Vishnu in Hinduism. It is located in Puri in the state of Odisha, situated on the eastern coast of India. As per temple records, King Indradyumna of Avanti built the main temple of Jagannath at Puri.

    • The 5 interesting facts about Jagannath Temple:

      • Flag Mystery: The temple's flag always flies in the opposite direction of the wind, a phenomenon seen as mysterious.
      • No Shadow: The temple's dome casts no shadow on the ground at any time of the day.
      • Prasadam Cooking: The food is cooked in seven stacked pots, with the top pot cooking first, defying normal cooking logic.
      • Birds Avoid the Temple: It is said that no birds or planes fly directly above the temple.
      • Nabakalebara Ritual: The temple idols are made of wood and are replaced every 12 or 19 years through a special ritual called Nabakalebara.
